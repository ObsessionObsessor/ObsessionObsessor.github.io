The Cold War

3. Why did the U.S. turn to military action to stop communist aggression in Korea?

Relationships between the US and the USSR, which were competing superpowers, had been worsening for some time during WWII, for depth, Joseph Stalin allying with Hitler before Hitler attacked the USSR, the United States refraining from attacking Germany after Germany attacked the USSR, and the United States keeping it’s development of the Atomic Bomb secret all contributed to worsening relationships. After WWII, the United Nations was used by the US and USSR to spread their influence over others. Finally, after Stalin refused to allow free elections, Truman was convinced that the United State’s and the USSR’s aims were at odds.
